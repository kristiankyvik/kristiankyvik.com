Practicing your product interview skills can not only prepare you for your next job hunt, but also help you sharpen your skills at your current role. I am working trough Exponent’s Complete PM Interview Course and here are my notes.

Structuring your answer

When answering any question, try to structure your answer using the template below.

1 - Listen: Take notes highlighting the main points

2 - Questions: Please restrain from answering question! Instead, aim to:

  • Scope the question
  • Clarify any vague point by asking questions
  • Re-phrase the question (e.g. does this sound ok?)

3 - Pause: Do not hesitate to spend at least 10-20 seconds before answering to think about the question

4 - Structure: As you pause, think about the structure. Invoke any frameworks (e.g. broad to deep). As an example, broad-to-deep lists possibilities and then picks one.

5 - Give your answer: Tell them what you think, give benchmarks and signposts, try to walk the interviewer through the answer.

6 - Pivot and check-in: Use the following questions:

  • Am I on the right track?
  • I decided not to touch a specific point. I could go into here, please let me know if I should go to more detail.
  • I realise that the direction I wanted to go is not the answer I want to continue for X and Y. Therefore I’d like to take a moment to re-consider and think about a new direction.

7 - Summary: Summarise and add caveats:

  • Qualitative tradeoffs - some pathological second order effects.
  • Second-hand effects…etc
  • Try to anticipate interviewer questions.
