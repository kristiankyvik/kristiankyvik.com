Compiled Life Advice from Hacker News Jul 21, 2022 (Updated: Jul 26, 2022) on Kristian Kyvik's blog

I decided to read a bunch of “life advice” threads from hacker news which I summarized below:

  • Do regular blood tests / healthchecks
  • Eat less
  • Maintenaince is harder than building things up - cultivate a habit of exercising early since this will only become - harder
  • Take good care of you teeth
  • Some fun activities include lifting, cyclying, surf, dancing…
  • Do stretch / pick up yoga
  • Fight the urge of becoming complacent - learn and try things personally and professionally
  • Set strict workhours - stress will greatly impact you mental and pysical health, it is rarely worth it
  • Have clear goals for yourself every month, year and decade
  • Minimize distractions - it is hard to overstate how important this is an how bad most people are at it
  • Conciously work to regret more things
  • Get good sleep - at least 8 hours
  • Find a good therapist
  • Avoid attaching your identity to people, their idea of who you are, youre job, or you posessions
  • Be a “bro” not a “bob” at work - get excited about things, don’t complain and do talk to the younger / newer people
  • Do medidate
  • Do exercise regularly - it will pay huge dividends later in life
  • Remember that at 30 you can still drop everything and go hike the Appalachian trail, at 40 that might not be possible - nor sound appealing
  • Love your boyfriend/girlfriend as much as you did when you where a teenager
  • Be a doer, not a talker
  • Wear earplugs at concerts
  • Realize how much incentives matter - everything is driven by “personal incentives” more so that “business incentives” - and these two aren’t usually aligned
  • Make the most out of your time with your parents
  • Marry someone “above your station” emotionally
  • Find the right set of people to surround yourself with and mantain these relationships
  • Keep track of your friends (and their contact details) and reach out to them often
  • Have parties
  • Dress nicely, not to impress but to not look like a slob
  • Pick one activity and commit to it for the next decade to get as good as you can
  • Get a good mouse and keyboard
  • Decide early whether you want kids
